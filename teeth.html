Teeth Whitening

Using remedies for teeth whitening is essential as they offer natural, non-abrasive solutions that brighten teeth without causing harm to enamel or gums, unlike some commercial whitening products. By harnessing the power of gentle yet effective ingredients, natural remedies can effectively remove stains and promote a brighter, healthier smile.

Note : Remember to apply small amount of any new ingredients on a tooth to ensure you don't have any adverse reactions.

Remedies for Teeth Whitening

Baking Soda and Lemon Paste

  • Mix 1 teaspoon of baking soda with enough fresh lemon juice to form a paste.
  • Apply the paste to your toothbrush and brush your teeth gently for about 1-2 minutes.
  • Rinse your mouth thoroughly with water

    • *Baking soda acts as a mild abrasive that can help remove surface stains, while lemon juice contains citric acid that can help whiten teeth. However, be cautious with this remedy as the acidity of lemon juice can erode tooth enamel over time, so use it sparingly and avoid using it if you have sensitive teeth or gum issues.

Activated Charcoal Paste

  • Wet your toothbrush and dip it into activated charcoal powder.
  • Brush your teeth gently for about 1-2 minutes.
  • Rinse your mouth thoroughly with water.

    • *Activated charcoal is highly absorbent and can help remove surface stains from the teeth. However, it's abrasive, so use it sparingly and avoid scrubbing too hard to prevent enamel damage.
